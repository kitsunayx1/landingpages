Why Veritas Platform exists

Veteran services run on
email, fax, and
missed deadlines.

Legal firms handling VA claims track NOD windows in spreadsheets. Medical providers manage DBQs across disconnected clinical systems. VSO representatives coordinate appeals without a shared timeline that every party can see.

The bigger problem is data. Organizations need access to veteran records to do their jobs — but once a veteran shares their records, they have no visibility into how long that data is retained, who accesses it, or whether it's ever deleted. Existing platforms treat veteran data as the organization's asset, not the veteran's.

Veritas Platform was built to solve both problems simultaneously: give organizations the workflow infrastructure they need, and give veterans explicit control over the data they share to get that help.

Context-aware workflows

The workflow engine generates step-by-step plans tailored to each organization's type and the specific case context. Legal cases get litigation steps. Medical cases get DBQ workflows. VSO cases get benefits navigation. Mental health codes and musculoskeletal codes trigger automatic adjustments.

Unified timeline

Every event on a case — VA submissions, C&P exams, decisions, correspondence, document receipts, deadlines — in one chronological view. The VA phase (pending, exam, awaiting decision, appeal) is automatically detected from the timeline.

Deadline tracking that doesn't slip

NOD windows, BVA appeal deadlines, evidence submission dates, SOC/SSOC deadlines. Status: upcoming, at-risk, overdue, completed. The deadlines a missed VA claims firm can never get back.

AI that respects data sensitivity

Organizations handling sensitive veteran data can run AI entirely on-premises via LM Studio — no cloud, no external API calls. Cloud providers (Anthropic, OpenAI-compatible) available with automatic failover. Your choice where processing happens.

Data sovereignty
Veterans control
what you receive.

Data arrives on the platform only through explicit veteran consent — not from a third-party data broker, not from a background sync. Every piece of veteran data has a retention policy and a scheduled deletion date enforced at the database layer, not just policy documents.

Workstreams

One platform.
Three workflows.

The workflow engine, UI, and tools adapt to your organization type. Legal firms, medical providers, and VSOs get purpose-built workflows — not a generic task manager they have to configure themselves.

Legal
VA Claims Firms

Litigation-focused workflows with evidence tracking, deadline management, and correspondence threading.

  • NOD, BVA, CAVC deadline tracker
  • Evidence review and legal triage workflow
  • Correspondence (VA letters, filings, calls)
  • ROM evidence flagging for musculoskeletal cases
  • Quality review step before submission
Medical
Medical Providers

Clinical documentation workflows built around the DBQ lifecycle and C&P exam coordination.

  • DBQ tracker (not_started → submitted)
  • C&P exam scheduling and prep workflow
  • Clinical review and evidence staging
  • Audiological and sleep study flagging
  • Medical evidence organization
VSO
Veteran Service Orgs

Benefits navigation and advocacy workflows with mental health awareness built in.

  • Benefits tracker across claim types
  • Needs assessment and benefits triage
  • Mental health documentation review (9xxx codes)
  • VSO guidance and documentation workflow
  • Appeal coordination
