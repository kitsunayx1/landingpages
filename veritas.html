Design principles

Built on a promise
to every veteran.

📱
Offline-first, always

Every feature works without internet. No network calls, no spinners, no "try again later." Your records are accessible regardless of connectivity.

🔒
Encrypted at rest

Sensitive fields use AES-GCM 256-bit encryption. Your PIN-derived key never touches disk. Even if your device is compromised, your data isn't readable without your PIN.

✋
You control sharing

Data only leaves your device when you tap Share. No background sync, no automatic uploads, no third-party data brokers. You decide what goes where.

🚫
No account required

No sign-up. No profile. No login. The app works completely without an account. There's no Veritas backend to send your data to even if we wanted to.

📊
No telemetry

No analytics. No crash reporting that phones home. No usage tracking. Optional local-only metrics if you want them — never transmitted.

💾
No data hostage

Downgrading to the free tier never deletes your records. You retain full local access to everything you've entered. Your data is always yours.

Why Veritas exists

The VA claims process
is hard enough
already.

Veterans navigating disability claims deal with lost records, missed deadlines, and organizations that need their data but give them no visibility into how it's used or how long it's kept. The tools that exist are either paper-based or cloud platforms that treat your medical history as their data asset.

Veritas was built by a veteran who went through the claims process and built the app they wished had existed. The premise is simple: your service records, your medical records, and your symptom logs belong on your device under your control — and you should be able to assemble a professional evidence packet without giving them to a third party first.

Evidence that holds up

Structured condition records, symptom logs with medical-grade fields, evidence file tagging, and PDF packet generation — all on device. Export a professional packet with SHA-256 integrity hashes.

Symptom logs that matter

Purpose-built log types for migraine, GERD, mental health, and generic conditions. Each captures the fields VA examiners actually care about. Logs automatically surface in your condition's evidence packet.

Understand your rating

The VA combined rating formula is confusing. Veritas includes an educational simulator that shows you step-by-step how individual ratings combine under the bilateral factor methodology.

Share on your terms

When you're ready to share with your attorney, VSO, or medical provider, Veritas generates a Shared Case bundle — a consented JSON package with an integrity manifest. You control what's included and when.

Our data promise
There is no Veritas backend.

This is not a marketing statement. There is no server that receives your data. No database that stores your conditions. No cloud that backs up your records. The app is a local tool running entirely on your device. If you want to understand exactly what Veritas does and doesn't do with your data, read the source code — it will be open for inspection.

Features

Every tool you need.
Nothing you don't.

📋 Conditions Ledger

Full CRUD for conditions with diagnostic codes, status, rating, timeline events, and evidence checklists seeded from 15 condition templates.

📝 Symptom Logging

Migraine, GERD, mental health, and generic log types. Encrypted sensitive fields. Date range filtering. Integrated into condition evidence packets.

📁 Evidence Vault

Import PDFs and images into a sandboxed evidence directory. Tag to conditions. Transactional deletion — file only removed if database succeeds.

📄 PDF Generation

Six export types: condition packet, lay statement, health context, migraine summary, full bundle ZIP, shared case bundle. All generated on-device.

❤️ Health Integration

Apple Health and Health Connect integration for sleep, resting HR, HRV, and steps. Summary-only, read-only. Layered into condition packets as context.

🧮 VA Math Simulator

Educational combined-rating calculator with step-by-step formula display. Save scenarios locally. Clear disclaimers — not a rating determination.
