Why Charter Exists

HOA software hasn't
changed since 2003.

The incumbent HOA platforms — CINC, Buildium, AppFolio — were built for large property management companies managing hundreds of associations. Your 80-unit community is an afterthought. You're paying enterprise pricing for software that requires professional training to operate.

The smaller tools are worse: a PDF form on a board member's personal laptop, a Google Sheet for dues tracking, and an email thread that nobody can find from 18 months ago when the roof leak happened.

Charter was built by someone who served on an HOA board and watched this happen firsthand. It's priced for a 40-unit community. It works without an IT department. And residents actually use the app because it's not an embarrassment.

Board Portal — Web
Run operations from a real dashboard.

Everything a board needs to manage an HOA — built for people who volunteer their time, not professional property managers.

  • Resident directory with unit management
  • Dues tracking and payment ledger
  • Violation lifecycle — notice to resolution
  • Maintenance queue with vendor assignment
  • Document library (CC&Rs, minutes, budgets)
  • Board meeting records and attendance
  • Announcement publishing with push + email
Resident App — Mobile

Residents actually use Charter because it gives them real-time visibility — not a login to check once a year when dues are due.

  • Announcement feed with push notifications
  • Submit and track maintenance requests
  • View violations and submit appeals
  • Check balance and payment history
  • Access community documents
  • Full English / Spanish support
